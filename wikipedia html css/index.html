The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth largest satellite in the Solar System. It is about one-quarter the size of Earth and the largest natural satellite relative to the size of its planet.

Overview

The Moon orbits Earth at an average distance of 384,400 km. It takes roughly 27.3 days to complete one orbit around Earth. Because of tidal locking, we always see the same side of the Moon from Earth.
